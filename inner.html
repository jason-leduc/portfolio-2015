Classic Movie Poster: Alien Isolation

In this project I wanted to include as many of the skills I have learned to date in Photoshop. Movie Poster designs are a perfect fit for a Photoshop project! They're chalk-full of photoshop layering and effects.

My Effort

This was not a project in which to be minimalist. I wanted to find a balance between overloading the piece with gratuitous Photoshop effects and artful use of the tools I learned to use effectively.

My major goal was to maintain editability

This was the main goal of this project. Making sure to isolate graphics on layers. Use masks to erase areas, not erase actual pixels, using Clipping Masks, etc.

Some editing was destructive, like use of the transform tool, for example. This is why I saved copies of all of your untouched images in a separate folder. Whenever possible, I duplicated the layer before I did a destructive transformation.

I wanted to make sure that my design looked like a movie piece with extraordinary visuals which people have come to expect from movie posters.

No matter what the subject was, I needed to design my own identity for it and make it appropriate and original.

I wanted to go in really heavily with the Photoshop effects but made sure that I did this in a considered way. Do not use effects for nothing. Make them appropriate to the movie.
